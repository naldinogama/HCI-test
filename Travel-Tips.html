Travel Tips for Visiting Angola

Prepare for your trip to Angola with our essential travel advice and local insights.

Best time to visit Languages Spoken: what is the currency? Transportation Guide: Is Angola safe? Some tips:
Best Time to Visit, is the dry season (May–October) is ideal for exploring Angola.
Clear skies, mild temperatures, and easier road travel make it perfect for safaris, city tours, and outdoor adventures.		 Portuguese is the official language, but major Local Languages: Umbundu, Kimbundu, Kikongo are widely spoken across the country.
In hotels, restaurants, and tourist areas, you’ll also find people who speak English.		 The local currency is the Angolan Kwanza (AOA).
Credit cards are accepted in larger establishments, but for markets and small purchases, it’s best to use cash or mobile payments.		 Angola’s major cities are connected by domestic flights, taxis, and intercity buses.
Car rentals with drivers are also available for comfortable long-distance travel.		 Angola is generally safe for visitors, but it’s wise to avoid isolated areas at night and keep valuables secure.
Always use trusted transport services and follow local advice.		 Protect yourself with sunscreen, bottled water, and make sure your vaccinations are up to date before traveling.
Pack light, breathable clothing for the tropical climate.
